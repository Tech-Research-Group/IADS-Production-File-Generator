		<title>General</title>
		<para>The Expeditionary Platoon Life Support Module (EPLSM) (<xref figid="G00003-10-5419-218-F0001"/>) consists of 8 subsystems that provide latrine, laundry, shower, dining, fuel, power, billeting, administration, storage facilities, and supplies for up to 75 personnel. The EPLSM theory of operation by subsystem is described in the following paragraphs.</para>

	<systhry>
		<title>Latrine Subsystem</title>
		<para>The Latrine Subsystem (<xref figid="G00003-10-5419-218-F0002"/>) consists of one Expeditionary Latrine System (ELS) that provides latrine facilities for male and female personnel. The ELS consists of four toilets, two urinals, and two sinks. For specific theory of operation related to the ELS, refer to <extref docno="TM 10-5419-209-13&amp;P"/>.</para>
		<para>The ELS contains a source water pump, wastewater pump, two water bags, and hoses to connect them.</para>

		</para>

		<para>Latrine Subsystem power connections (<xref figid="G00003-10-5419-218-F0003"/>) are available at the Power Generation and Fuel Distribution Subsystem. Additional power cables are provided to connect the ELS to the power distribution box (PDB).</para>

		<title>Kitchen/Dining Subsystem</title>
		<para>The Kitchen/Dining Subsystem (<xref figid="G00003-10-5419-218-F0007"/>) consists of one Expeditionary TRICON Kitchen System (ETKS) complexed to a Type-37 TEMPER shelter with an environmental control unit (ECU) that functions as a climate-controlled dining facility (DFAC). The DFAC has 8 tables, 16 benches, a beverage station, and an ice maker (not shown). A TRICON Refrigerated Container System (TRCS) is located with the ETKS for refrigeration of food supplies. Two 3,000-gal (11,356-L) collapsible fabric water bags supply ETKS source water and hold wastewater (SOURCE water bag and WASTE water bag).</para>

		<title>Power Generation and Fuel Distribution Subsystem</title>
		<para>The Power Generation and Fuel Distribution Subsystem consists of three 60-kW generators, three PDBs, two 1,000-gal (3,785-L) fuel tanks with a spill containment system, a Fuel Distribution System (FDS), nozzle stand, fuel hoses, and hose connection fittings. The FDS and nozzle stand are used as a retail fuel point. The potential for any fuel spillage is mitigated by the use of spill containment systems on the generators, fuel tank, and FDS. The FDS is powered by an extension cord connected to one generator.</para>
		<para>For specific theory of operation related to the various components of the Power Generation and Fuel Distribution Subsystem, refer to the following TMs:<randlist bullet="no">

	<systhry>
		<title>Transportation/Storage Subsystem</title>
		<para>The Transportation/Storage Subsystem (<xref figid="G00003-10-5419-218-F0017"/>) consists of nine storage and shipping TRICONs. Each TRICON container may contain an internal shipping and storage shelf, shoring beams, wooden braces, and packout pallets depending on container designation. Ratchet straps are included with each container in order to secure contents during transport. The Transportation/Storage Subsystem is used to pack out the contents of the EPLSM in preparation for movement to a new location. For specific theory of operation related to the storage TRICONs, refer to <extref docno="TM 55-8145-236-13&amp;P"/>.</para>

		<title>Electrical Power Connections Layout</title>
		<para>The electrical power connections layout (<xref figid="G00003-10-5419-218-F0050"/>) is designed to aid the user setting up the electrical layout of the EPLSM. There are many options for how to place the cables. Without correct placement of the cables, harm may come to cables or personnel in the camp. Always place power cables over water hoses. Cables may be routed differently if the camp requires an alternative layout.
