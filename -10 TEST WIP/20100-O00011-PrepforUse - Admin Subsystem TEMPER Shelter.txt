				<step1>
					<specpara>
						<note>
							<trim.para>In low light conditions, floodlights from TRICON 50C can be assembled and used IAW <xref wpid="O00019-10-5419-218"/>.</trim.para>
						</note>
						<para>Measure 240 ft (73.1 m) along long edge of site from site reference point marked in <xref wpid="O00002-10-5419-218"/> and 70 ft (21.3 m) along short edge of site. Mark that point with a flag as starting point for deploying Administration Subsystem (<xref figid="O00011-10-5419-218-F0001"/>).</para>
					</specpara>

					<specpara>
						<warning>
							<icon-set boardno="Heavy_Object"/>
							<icon-set boardno="Heavy_Parts-above"/>
							<icon-set boardno="Heavy_Parts-wall"/>
							<icon-set boardno="Ear_Protection_Symbol"/>
							<trim.para>Some Administration Subsystem components are heavy. Solar shade bag weighs in excess of 50 lb (23 kg) and require two personnel to lift. Each 60-A, 50-ft power cable weighs 50 lb (22.7 kg) and 60-A, 100-ft power cable weighs 100 lb (45.4 kg) requiring two personnel to lift. TEMPER shelter and environmental control unit (ECU) must be moved using MHE. ECU should not be attempted to move by hand. Personnel must not stand between MHE and item or below item while it is being moved. All safety precautions must be followed, and proper lifting technique must be used. Failure to comply may result in injury to personnel. Seek immediate medical attention if injury occurs.</trim.para>
							<trim.para>Hearing can be permanently damaged if personnel are exposed to constant high noise levels of 85 decibels (dB) or greater. Wear approved hearing protection devices when operating or working within 61 ft (19 m) of MHE when engine is running. Personnel exposed to high noise levels shall participate in a hearing conservation program in accordance with <extref docno="DA PAM 40-501"/>. Hearing loss occurs gradually but becomes permanent over time. Seek immediate medical attention if injury occurs.</trim.para>
						</warning>
						<note>
							<trim.para>Ground rod kits, compressors, first aid kits, marking flags, air gun, air compressor reducers and couplings, air lines, sledgehammers, jack, rake, drum repair kit, reciprocating saw and blades, sand bags, shovel, slide hammer, stepladder, antiseizing tape, flagging tape, measuring tape, and tool kit are setup items and do not need to be unpacked if they are already available.</trim.para>
							<trim.para>All hoses and pipe fittings; 2 brooms; 3 dustpans; 1 environmental control unit (ECU); 9 60-A, 100-ft power cables; 2 60-A, 50-ft power cables;  4 removable floor sections; interior and exterior ISO end liners; 12 tie down straps, and all parts kits are supplied as spares and may remain in TRICONs indefinitely or be stored in trunk lockers once administration TEMPER shelter is set up.</trim.para>
							<trim.para>Morale, welfare, and recreation items such as dice, frisbees, playing cards, and board games may remain in TRICON until administration TEMPER shelter is set up. These items will be stored in trunk lockers.</trim.para>
							<trim.para>Insulated liner and 14-fixture light set are installed in rolled up TEMPER shelter.</trim.para>
							<trim.para>Components of End Item (COEI) List (<xref wpid="S00002-10-5419-218"/>) can be utilized to identify items required.</trim.para>
						</note>
						<para>Unpack TRICONs 50C, N51A-1, N51A-2, N51A-3, N51A-4, N51B, N55B, N55C, and 57D IAW <xref wpid="O00005-10-5419-218"/> to locate and retrieve components listed in <xref tableid="O00011-10-5419-218-T0001"/>.</para>
